The problem

Voice chat treats every sound equally. People don't.

In a team call, four people can be talking at once. Existing audio tools like AGC, noise suppression and manual volume sliders process every voice the same way. They amplify the room. They can't tell you that someone is talking to you.

Important callouts get lost. Coordination breaks down. You miss the moment your teammate said your name.

How it works

Three moments. Invisible to you.

Clarion runs silently in the background. When it matters, it acts. You never touch a slider.

01 - Transcribe
"Hey Alex, push mid-"
Clarion streams live audio from every participant through Deepgram's speech recognition. Every word, in real time, with speaker attribution.
02 - Detect
Your name. Found.
The transcript is scanned for your name on every new word. The detection is local to your client so no one else sees or hears anything change.
03 - Amplify
Only for you.
The room's volume is adjusted, just on your device, just for that moment. The rest of the room stays unchanged. You never miss the callout that mattered.
